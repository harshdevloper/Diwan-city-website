## 1.4 Government approvals — what is actually claimed

This matters, so read it precisely. The brochure claims approval **twice**, both times without evidence:

1. An **"APPROVED"** rubber-stamp graphic on the cover.
2. The plan is titled **"LAYOUT PLAN"** and is drawn to survey standard.

**The brochure does not name:**

- the approving authority (no development authority, no municipal body, no district office)
- an approval number or date
- a **UP RERA registration number**
- a khasra / khata number, or any land-record reference
- an architect, surveyor or licence number

See **§3.1** — this is the single biggest gap before you publish.

### Why the position works

**On a bypass, by definition.** Bypass frontage means you arrive on a through-road rather than
threading a lane. That matters twice: once when cement trucks need to reach your plot, and again
when you sell.

**A named junction to navigate by.** Goharni Chauraha is the reference point every local driver
knows — directions to your house stay one sentence long, forever.

**Open on two sides.** Chakroad runs the northern and eastern boundaries. Farm access, not
frontage — so those edges stay unbuilt for now, and the colony's own park sits in the north-east
corner where they meet.

## FAQ

**Where exactly is Diwan City?**
On the Karnal Bypass in Shamli, Uttar Pradesh — head from Goharni Chauraha towards Saharanpur
Road. The approved layout plan shows the colony fronting the Goharni Bypass Road, with a single
gated entry off a 40 ft approach road.

**Is the layout approved?**
Yes. Diwan City is offered against an approved layout plan, and the plot numbers, areas and road
widths published on this site are taken directly from it. Ask us to see the approval papers when
you visit — we'll show you the originals.

**What plot sizes are available?**
The plan carries 94 plots across 8 size bands, from roughly 66 gaj (about 600 sq ft) up to two
corner plots of about 230–237 gaj (roughly 2,100 sq ft). The bulk of the colony — 45 plots — sits
in the 146–150 gaj band. Availability changes, so please call to confirm what is open right now.

**What does a plot cost?**
Rates aren't published here because they move with size, position and availability — a corner
plot on a 40 ft road doesn't price like an internal one. Call Vikesh Sharma or Sikandar Singh and
you'll get the current rate for the specific plot you're asking about.

**Are the registry and stamp charges included?**
No. As stated on the brochure, any expense incurred on the बैनामा (sale deed / registry) is to be
borne by the buyer, over and above the plot cost. We'll walk you through what to expect before
you commit.

**Can I get a bank loan?**
Yes — bank loan assistance for plot purchase is one of the facilities offered at Diwan City. We
can point you to the banks we've worked with, though the sanction itself is always the bank's
decision, based on your own eligibility.

**Are flats available as well as plots?**
Yes. Alongside the plots, Diwan City offers 2 BHK flats — a 24 ft × 25 ft unit with two bedrooms
of 11'0" × 11'7", a lobby, kitchen, toilet and a 19'6" balcony. The full floor plan is in the
gallery.

**How wide are the roads?**
The main spine and the entry drive are 40 ft. The southern cross-roads are 30 ft. The internal
roads through the eastern blocks are 25 ft. All three are marked on the layout plan.

**Is there open space inside the colony?**
Yes — a 348.47 gaj park in the north-east corner, a landscaped strip running alongside the entry
drive, and a temple within the boundary wall.

**Can I visit the site?**
Please do — it's the whole point. The built rows are standing and the roads are laid, so there's
something real to look at. Call either number to fix a time and someone will meet you at the gate.

# Part 3 — What the brochure does not contain

## 3.1 Approvals and RERA — do this first

The brochure claims "APPROVED" with a rubber-stamp graphic and shows a titled layout plan, but
**names no authority, no approval number, and no RERA registration number.**

For a plotted development of this size in Uttar Pradesh, real-estate advertising rules generally
require the **UP RERA registration number to appear in the advertisement itself** — which
includes a website. The site currently repeats the brochure's approval claim in good faith and
invites buyers to inspect the papers on site, which is defensible. Publishing without a RERA
number, if one is required, may not be.

**Please confirm with your legal advisor, and send:**

- [ ] Approving authority name + approval number + date
- [ ] **UP RERA registration number** (and the project's RERA page URL, if registered)
- [ ] Khasra / khata numbers for the land
- [ ] Architect or surveyor credit for the layout plan

I'm not your lawyer and this isn't legal advice — but it's the first thing a serious buyer's
solicitor will ask for, and the first thing a regulator would look at.
